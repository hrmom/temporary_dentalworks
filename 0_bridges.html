Why Receive Dental Bridges?

Three or more crowns put together make up a dental bridge. The hardest crown out of the three is used to replace the tooth thatâ€™s missing. The two teeth that surround the gap act as anchors for the dental bridge.

A dental bridge is a restorative method used to bring back or enhance the function of your teeth. Although dental bridges also help cosmetically, filling in gaps helps prevent poor oral health. In addition to improving your oral health, replacing missing teeth will prevent your remaining teeth from shifting and prevent your jaw bone from deteriorating.

Traditional Dental Bridges

There a lot of different types of bridges and the one that suits your needs depends on the location of the gap as well as the number of missing teeth. Traditional dental bridges consist of one more artificial teeth supported by dental crowns. This type of bridge can replace teeth anywhere in the mouth. The number of replacement teeth can vary however, the highest amount of teeth a bridge can replace is a 6-unit bridge. An important factor to consider when receiving a dental bridge is that your dentist will need to remove the enamel of your natural tooth in order to replace it with a crown. How is the Recovery Process?

The recovery process for dental bridges is smooth and fast. Unlike other surgical procedures, you are able to go about your everyday activities within a day or so!

Some side effects patients experience include soreness and tenderness in the mouth. This is a result of the dentist grinding down your teeth. Tenderness and bleeding of the gums is also another side effect that people experience after the procedure. Within a few days, the soreness will go away but the gums may require a few weeks to heal completely.

If you choose to be sedated, you should avoid driving right after the procedure. In order to ensure your dental bridges remain in good shape:

  • - Visit your dentist near you twice a year for a check-up
  • - Be educated on what foods to avoid
  • - Practice good oral hygiene
